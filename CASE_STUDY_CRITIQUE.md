## 1. MISSING: Business Impact & Metrics

**Current State:** No quantitative outcomes or business metrics  
**Why It Matters:** Senior roles require proof of ROI and business value  
**Industry Standard:** Top portfolios lead with metrics (e.g., "Increased conversion by 23%", "Reduced support tickets by 40%")

### What to Add:
- **Before/After Metrics:**
  - Conversion rates
  - Time-to-complete (onboarding, applications)
  - Error rates (operational tools)
  - Customer satisfaction scores (NPS, CSAT)
  - Cost savings (operational efficiency)
  - Compliance audit pass rates

- **Example Framing:**
  > "Reduced onboarding abandonment from 45% to 18% while maintaining 100% KYC compliance, resulting in $2.3M additional annual revenue."

---

## 2. MISSING: Discovery & Research Phase

**Current State:** Approach jumps straight to solution  
**Why It Matters:** Shows strategic thinking and rigor before design  
**Industry Standard:** Top agencies spend 30-40% of case study on discovery

### What to Add:
- **User Research:**
  - User interviews (how many, what insights)
  - Survey data
  - Behavioral analytics
  - Pain point mapping

- **Competitive Analysis:**
  - How competitors handle similar problems
  - Market gaps identified
  - Differentiation strategy

- **Stakeholder Alignment:**
  - Workshops conducted
  - Cross-functional collaboration
  - Risk/compliance team involvement

## Recommended Case Study Structure

### For Senior Product Design Manager Level:

```
1. Executive Summary (1-2 paragraphs)
   - Client/Project context
   - Your role
   - Key achievement (with metric if possible)

2. The Challenge (2-3 sections)
   - Business problem
   - User problem
   - Regulatory/technical constraints
   - Current state metrics (baseline)

3. Discovery & Research (3-4 sections)
   - User research (interviews, surveys, analytics)
   - Competitive analysis
   - Stakeholder workshops
   - Key insights synthesized

4. Strategy & Approach (2-3 sections)
   - Design principles established
   - Information architecture
   - User journey mapping
   - Design system approach

5. Design & Iteration (3-4 sections)
   - Early concepts
   - Key design decisions (with rationale)
   - Iterations based on feedback
   - Final design system

6. Implementation (1-2 sections)
   - Developer handoff
   - Quality assurance
   - Launch process

7. Results & Impact (2-3 sections)
   - Quantitative metrics (before/after)
   - Qualitative feedback
   - Business impact
   - Lessons learned

8. Reflection (1 section)
   - What worked well
   - What would you do differently
   - Future opportunities
```
